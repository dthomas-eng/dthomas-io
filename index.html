Micro-Prism Alignment System

Tektronix

I was brought in to address a bottleneck in the production line of a flagship opto-electronics product. As orders increased, a cruicial step involving the gluing of a micro-scale prism to the end of a fiber optic cable requiring precise positioning began to cause slowdowns and significant scrap.

Instead of building a second work station and doubling the labor to meet demand (as initially proposed by management), I performed a full process analysis and proposed a redesign with improvements that would allow a throughput increase without adding labor. My proposal was accepted and I was granted a small budget.

To keep cost down and arrive at an optimal soution, I used 3D printing to mock and test mechanical components. I took a highly iterative approach to design, collecting feedback from manufaturing technicians at each step. When nesscessary big-ticket items threatened to bust the budget, I designed and built those items in-house.

Successful completion of this 6-month project led to 20% reduction in scrap for a $10M/year military avionics product with no need for additional labor. The project was completed one month ahead of schedule, using 97% of the alotted budget.

Airbus STC Analysis

Altech Aerospace

I was tapped to author a 300-page analysis validating a major alteration to the recently released Airbus A330-900neo. Client time constraints required delivery within 2 months from the project start date. A typical timeframe for similar analyses is 6 months.

I combined my knowledge of traditional aircraft structural analysis with software engineering skills to automate the most time-intensive steps of report generation to deliver a thorough analyis of the structure ahead of the deadline.

The report was accepted by the FAA with only one round of minor edits, indicating a complete and well-organized analysis. Altech went on to win additional contracts with a major client due to my on-time delivery.
